Distributed multi-quadrotors system formation control

Release time：2023-04-14

Introduction

    With the diversification and complexity of application scenarios, a single four-rotor UAV has a single structure, which makes it difficult to perform tasks efficiently. At the same time, there are other problems in many aspects: (1) the efficiency of a single UAV to perform tasks is low;(2) The type and quantity of the equipment carried is small, and its role is very limited;(3) The endurance of a single UAV is poor.In view of these limitations, in the face of high risk and high dynamic environment, UAV has been transformed from a single aircraft to a multi-aircraft "cluster". In the face of uncertain future scenarios, multi-aircraft cooperation can make up for each other, improve the diversity and complexity of task execution, and focus on improving the overall efficiency of task execution. On the other hand, multi-aircraft cooperation can also realize AD hoc networking, in which single aircraft can perceive the task environment and exchange information independently. Meanwhile, multi-aircraft cooperation not only relies on individual power, but also can carry out the task even if individual is damaged or destroyed in an emergency.The UAV needs multi-aircraft cooperative control to realize the cluster. Multi-aircraft cooperative control can not only exert the agility of single aircraft, but also realize the overall efficiency, so as to achieve the predetermined task.Therefore, the cooperative strategy of multi-quadrotor UAV emerges. Multi-aircraft cooperation Multiple simple UAVs are added to the task, which can carry sensors with multiple functions, achieve resource complementarity and information fusion, increase the group's information perception ability, reduce the task time, and improve the execution efficiency.Multi-uav collaboration technology is also a hot research topic in recent years, including formation control, target allocation, trajectory planning and so on.Formation control, as a basic technology of multi-aircraft cooperation, aims to make each UAV maintain or transform in multiple formations under the premise of satisfying the given geometric relationship, which can provide more stable performance for difficult tasks.In addition, the cooperative control of multiple UAV systems cannot be achieved without the communication network, which requires multiple UAV systems to realize dynamic networking when executing flight tasks such as formation, so that different task information or UAV flight status information can be transmitted in real time in the communication network to ensure the completion of the final task.It is obvious that the communication network will also have an impact on formation of formation, but most traditional formation control methods ignore this point, so how to use the status information obtained from the communication network to design formation controller is an urgent problem to be solved in engineering practice.With the rapid development of computer and network communication technology, flight formation control based on consistency has attracted wide attention.Consistency refers to the use of neighbor information in the communication network, each individual build a suitable controller, and finally make all or part of the state quantity of multi-agent system tend to be the same.In this distributed formation mode, it is necessary to form some feasible communication topology between UAVs to realize the overall formation flight control.

    As an important research field of multi-intelligent system unity, the consistency theory provides a good idea and a new direction for solving the problem of multi-UAV formation under different communication networks.For fixed formation, the control objective of multiple UAVs can be considered as reaching the same speed, and the offset is increased on the basis of reaching the same position.So formation and maintenance of formation can be essentially transformed into consistency problem.
