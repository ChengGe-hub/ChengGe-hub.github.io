  • Coordinated tracking and landing by UAV and UGV

The landing of the quadrotor on the vehicle platform

Introduction

    Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) and unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs) are two types of robotic technologies that can work together to accomplish a variety of tasks. One such task is coordinated landing, where a UAV and UGV work in tandem to safely guide the UAV down to a landing zone on the ground. The process usually begins with the UAV hovering over the landing zone while the UGV moves into position beneath. The UGV then deploys a platform or net, which serves as a target for the UAV to land on. Using sensors and control systems, the UAV adjusts its altitude and angle to approach and align itself with the target. As it descends, the UGV guides and stabilizes the platform or net, ensuring a smooth and safe landing.

    Coordinated landing has many potential applications, including package delivery, reconnaissance, and search and rescue missions. By working together, UAVs and UGVs can increase the precision and safety of their operations, as well as reduce the workload for human operators.

Experiment 1

    Firstly, the location information of unmanned vehicles and drones can be obtained through motion capture systems and reflective spheres. By designing a consistent cooperative controller, appropriate forces or torques are given to unmanned vehicles and drones to achieve convergence in horizontal plane position. Next, a judgment module is designed to lock the current height when the Manhattan distance of the position is less than a threshold, and fit a descending curve from the previous height to the horizontal plane for altitude adjustment. At this point, the horizontal position will always remain stable.
