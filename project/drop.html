  • Coordinated tracking and landing by UAV and UGV

Learning and perception of MATLAB

Release time：2023-03-14

Unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) and unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs) are two types of robotic technologies that can work together to accomplish a variety of tasks. One such task is coordinated landing, where a UAV and UGV work in tandem to safely guide the UAV down to a landing zone on the ground.

The process usually begins with the UAV hovering over the landing zone while the UGV moves into position beneath. The UGV then deploys a platform or net, which serves as a target for the UAV to land on. Using sensors and control systems, the UAV adjusts its altitude and angle to approach and align itself with the target. As it descends, the UGV guides and stabilizes the platform or net, ensuring a smooth and safe landing.

Coordinated landing has many potential applications, including package delivery, reconnaissance, and search and rescue missions. By working together, UAVs and UGVs can increase the precision and safety of their operations, as well as reduce the workload for human operators.
